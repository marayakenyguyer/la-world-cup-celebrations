About the Kick It In the Park Program

The World Cup
in Every Neighborhood

Kick It In the Park, a partnership between Council Districts, Recreation & Parks, and the Mayor's Office, ensures the World Cup reaches every corner of the city — from the San Fernando Valley to the Harbor, from Westside parks to East LA recreation centers.

These free, family-friendly events transform City of LA Recreation & Parks sites into vibrant gathering spaces where Angelenos watch the matches on large LED screens surrounded by youth soccer clinics or youth fun zones, neighborhood programming, and community resources. No ticket, no cost, no barriers.

🗺️
Citywide Reach

Sites span across Council Districts, from Sylmar and Northridge to Wilmington and Venice Beach.

🔄
Rotating Schedule

2–3 Rec & Park sites activated each match day, with each participating Council District hosting 7 celebrations across the tournament.

🌟
Testing for the Future

This is a chance to pilot what celebrations might look like during the 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games. We're eager to get your feedback!

June 11 – July 19

Tournament dates for the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

Free for All

No tickets, no registration required. Just show up, bring your blankets and chairs, and enjoy the game with your neighbors.

What's at Every Celebration

What to Expect at Kick It In the Park Events

Each Council District may put their own spin on the Kick It In the Park events, with local activations, vendors, and experiences. Each day may look a little different, but it will always feature the World Cup games!

📺

Live Match Screenings

Large mobile LED screens broadcast every World Cup match in real-time so you don't miss a goal.

⚽

PlayLA Soccer Clinics

Free youth soccer mini-clinics and skill activities at select sites run by the City's PlayLA program for kids of all ages.

🎉

Family Fun Zones

Interactive activities, local park stations, and family-friendly programming.

🏛️

City Services & Resources

Resource tables connecting residents with City departments, community organizations, and local services.

Bring your own blankets, chairs, and snacks. All events are free and open to the public, up to 1,000 people at a time.

Getting to the Celebrations

Most celebrations are free and open to the public — and the best way to get there is without a car. Here's how we recommend making your way to the party.

🚲

Bike There

Riding is a great way to get to your local watch party. Metro Bike Share stations are located across L.A. — grab a bike near you and ride over.
